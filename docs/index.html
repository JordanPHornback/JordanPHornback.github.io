"The whole is greater than the sum of the parts."

- Aristotle

I have only recently taken my first steps into programming, but I can see that it's a field with extremely wide application. Some of our adventures as software engineers will lead us to building entire new apps, new games, or new websites, which may sound like too big of an undertaking. Fortunately though, now that I have begun to learn, I can see those promethean sized projects are built one piece at a time, like building legos or piecing together a puzzle. Add one function here. Center a div there. Link the stylesheet. Suddenly like magic, the whole becomes greater than the sum of it's parts.

I think this quote fits well to not only the large projects that might be headed out way, but also to the roles we will fill where maybe our responsiblities are only to the UI of a single page. Maybe one day we find ourselves as hotshot programmers at Google, Meta, Apple, or anywhere else, and our job is to design a submit button on a webpage. That contribution might be considered "small", but if you look at the whole of the webpage or the company, our "small" contribution has afforded end-users something that they need.

About Me

You can find out more in my experience section, but a short summary of my work experience and interests would be that I have a passion for safety. Motivation

As I mentioned before, I just recently became a father! My son, the oldest, was born in 2021, and his little sister a year later in 2022. Becoming a father has wildly changed my goals in life. Before my kids came around, I didn't mind taking on the overnight shifts and sleeping all day or working long hours that would have me gone 80+ hours a week. Driving to the office wasn't a problem, and I was more than happy to be in management leading a team.

Now that I have my children though, I want to be able to spend more time with my family. My experience now as a software engineer has been amazing for my work life balance. The flexibility of working towards a goal instead of working set hours has been a life saver. Being able to finish a task and step away to make lunch for the family and pick right back up when I'm done is a miracle. Putting the kids down for a nap and being able to jump into my work for the next day, or having something I can work on during middle of the night feedings has helped me to be not only a more active parent, but also a more productive worker.

Goals

A goal I have for my career in software engineering would be to be on a team that handles cyber security. With so much of our lives now relying on technology, I want to be able to help protect my children and the next generation from cyber attacks that might endanger their safety or identities. The scale isn't the most important thing to me, whether it's security for a social media app where they post pictures of their lives, or maybe on a broader scale like a government position protecting from infrastucture attacks, I'd like to know my work helped to make people safer.
